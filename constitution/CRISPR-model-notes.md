# CRISPR Model — Reference Notes

> Reference document for the spawn/edit/retire cell lifecycle that underpins JSF governance.
> See `constitution/v0.md` for the authoritative JSF adaptation.

## Origin

The CRISPR model was first implemented at IIIT Nagpur as a self-governing student tech organization — Central Research Initiative and Student Public Relations — founded by Krishna Chaudhari. By handover, CRISPR IIIT Nagpur had shipped 20+ projects serving ~2,200 students across 5 departments.

JSF replicates this model for IIM Calcutta: small autonomous project cells that carry institutional DNA forward across graduating cohorts.

## JSF Primitives

- **Founding Cell (Cell 0):** Co-founders hold constitutional DNA. Cannot be dissolved.
- **Project Cell:** Self-managing cell with cell lead, backlog, release authority. Ships independently within JSF constitution.
- **Alumni Intake:** Contributor → Committer → Cell Governance via contribution threshold.
- **Cross-cell Guilds:** Design Guild, Backend Guild etc. span cells; knowledge carriers across cohorts.
- **Retirement = Archival:** Retired cells move to `archive/`, slug burned, retrospective written.

## Why This Works for IIMC

Students graduate every 2 years. Without a contribution-gated succession model, projects die with their founders. The CRISPR model makes succession structural: you earn your way into governance, then hand it to the next cohort the same way.
